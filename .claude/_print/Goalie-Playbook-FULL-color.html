A

Tracking & No-Flinch

Brave eyes first — and hunt the rebound.

A1

Bucket Toss Tracking

Eyes lock on and follow the ball all the way in. Zero pressure.

Goal

  • Skill: eyes track the ball through the catch
  • Why: build tracking confidence early
  • Watch: quiet head, no blinking, no turning away

Setup & Ball

Goalie kneels (no stick) 6–8 ft from coach. Coach underhand-tosses tennis balls to the hands/chest, announced every time.

2-Person

Backup goalie / parent: shags and refills the bucket, calls “TRACK IT!” every toss, swaps in as kneeling goalie every 10 reps. Nobody idle.

BeginnerSoft tosses to the hands, announced. Catch two-handed and say “got it.”
AdvancedVary height; catch one-handed. On a miss, chase the loose ball and scoop it (first rebound hunt).

Mastery Ladder

L1 · IntroductionKeeps eyes on the ball through the catch, doesn’t turn his head away
L2 · CompetentTracks and catches calmly without flinching, says “got it” on his own
L3 · Game ReadyCatches ~8 of 10 while stepping to the ball, eyes never leave it

Coaching Cues

Eyes In!Track It!Hunt It!
A2

The Bounce Wall

Get comfortable with a ball moving at him and reacting to a bounce.

Goal

  • Skill: react to a moving, bouncing ball
  • Why: comfort with a ball coming at the body
  • Watch: steps to it, stick down, body stays square

Setup & Ball

Goalie in net with stick. Coach 10–12 ft out rolls/one-bounces a Swax Lax at the feet and lower body. Low and slow — stepping, not diving.

2-Person

Backup goalie / parent: stands behind the net as a “ball stopper” so misses don’t roll away. Rotate goalies every 8 shots.

BeginnerSlow ground rolls at the feet. Step and trap, stick head down.
AdvancedOne-hop bounces to either side at moderate pace. Stick AND body behind it.

Mastery Ladder

L1 · IntroductionSteps toward the ball instead of standing still
L2 · CompetentStays square and traps low rolls without backing up
L3 · Game ReadyHandles one-hop bounces both sides, body behind it, ~7 of 10

Coaching Cues

Stick Down!Step & Stop!Stay Square!
A3

Rapid-Fire Hands

No-flinch reflex — get a stick up fast, repeatedly, eyes open.

Goal

  • Skill: quick hands, no-flinch reflex
  • Why: kill the blink before it becomes a habit
  • Watch: eyes stay open, resets between reps

Setup & Ball

Goalie close, 6 ft, with stick. Coach rapid-tosses soft tennis balls to alternating sides. Chest-height or lower at 6 ft — a mask shot this close trains the blink.

2-Person

Backup goalie / parent: becomes the feeder, handing the coach two balls at a time so the pace never stops. Swap each round.

Beginner3 tosses in a row, slow, announced “right… left… right.”
Advanced5–6 unannounced rapid tosses. Head-height ONLY after backing to 10–12 ft. Reset to ready each time.

Mastery Ladder

L1 · IntroductionGets the stick to most balls, eyes stay open
L2 · CompetentNo flinch on rapid alternating tosses, resets to ready
L3 · Game ReadyTracks 5–6 in a row eyes-open, calm hands, ~8 of 10 touched

Coaching Cues

Eyes Open!Hands Up!Reset Fast!
B

Arc & Stepping

Attack the ball. The save is the feet.

B1

The Arc Walk

Stay on the curved top of the crease — always between ball and net.

Goal

  • Skill: hold the crease arc, stay square
  • Why: be in the right spot before the shot
  • Watch: feet don’t cross, stays square to the ball

Setup & Ball

Lay 3–5 cones in a shallow arc across the top of the crease. No ball. Goalie shuffles cone to cone staying square to a coach walking side to side.

2-Person

Backup goalie / parent: stands at the far pipe and points to the coach, giving a target to stay square to. Rotate every 5 trips.

BeginnerSlow walk cone to cone, stick up, never crossing feet. Coach calls “left… right…”.
AdvancedCoach moves random and faster; shuffle to match, freeze square on the whistle. Remove cones.

Mastery Ladder

L1 · IntroductionShuffles cone to cone without crossing his feet
L2 · CompetentStays square to a slow-moving coach along the arc
L3 · Game ReadyMatches a faster random-moving coach and freezes square on command

Coaching Cues

Top of Arc!Don't Cross!Stay Square!
B2

Step & Attack the ShotSTEP

Attack the ball — step toward the shot. The “T” happens on its own when he moves right.

Goal

  • Skill: aggressive step toward the ball
  • Why: saves come from attacking, not reaching
  • Watch: lead foot and hands go AT the ball, nose follows

Setup & Ball

Goalie in ready stance. Coach 10 ft out calls a side, lobs a Swax Lax high and wide. Goalie steps toward the ball and drives the hands at it. The back foot forms a “T” naturally — don’t coach the T, coach the attack.

2-Person

Backup goalie / parent: drops a cone to the side the goalie should attack, giving a target. Swap shooter/goalie every 8 reps.

BeginnerSlow announced lobs to one side. Just step toward the ball, don’t reach from flat feet.
AdvancedUnannounced high/wide both sides. Hands lead, lead foot drives, save at full extension.

Mastery Ladder

L1 · IntroductionSteps toward the ball instead of reaching from flat feet
L2 · CompetentAttacks the shot with hands and lead foot on unannounced shots, squares up
L3 · Game ReadyDrives to high/wide shots and saves ~half of them, nose to the ball

Coaching Cues

Step to the Ball!Attack the Shot!Push, Don't Reach!Nose to Ball!
B3

High-Low-Side Reaction

Match the right move to each of the four save zones, automatically.

Goal

  • Skill: pick the right move per save zone
  • Why: the body learns where to go without thinking
  • Watch: correct move per zone, resets to ready

Setup & Ball

Goalie in net, stick up. Coach 12 ft out names a zone, tosses a Swax Lax there. Step up for high, drop hands for low, step to the ball for wide.

2-Person

Backup goalie / parent: calls the zone aloud a half-second before the coach throws, training ears and reaction. Rotate every 10 reps.

BeginnerOne zone at a time, repeated. Master high, then low, then sides — separately.
AdvancedRandom zones, no warning. Pick the correct move on the fly, reset to ready.

Mastery Ladder

L1 · IntroductionMakes the right move when the zone is called first
L2 · CompetentPicks the correct move on most unannounced single shots
L3 · Game ReadyReads random zones and resets each time, right move ~7 of 10

Coaching Cues

Match the Zone!Hands Lead!Back to Ready!
C

Save, Rebound & Wide Clear

Finish the save and get it out wide.

C1

Save, Clamp & Rebound HuntREBOUND

Secure it — and if it pops loose, attack the rebound instantly.

Goal

  • Skill: secure the save, then hunt loose balls
  • Why: the save isn’t over until the ball is his
  • Watch: clamps it, chases pop-loose, never admires the stop

Setup & Ball

Goalie in net. Coach lobs a soft Swax Lax to the stick — save, squeeze (clamp), cradle. Sometimes the coach throws one that pops loose; goalie chases the rebound and scoops it.

2-Person

Backup goalie / parent: stands 5 ft to the side as an outlet target; once secured/hunted, goalie tosses to the helper. Rotate every 8 reps.

BeginnerCatch and freeze — hold the clamp two seconds, show the ball is secured.
AdvancedMix in pop-loose saves. React to the bounce, scoop the loose ball, then outlet.

Mastery Ladder

L1 · IntroductionMakes the save and holds it (clamps) instead of dropping it
L2 · CompetentReacts to a pop-loose ball and chases it instead of admiring the stop
L3 · Game ReadySecures or re-scoops ~7 of 10, then outlets, in a live rep

Coaching Cues

Save & Squeeze!Hunt the Rebound!Scoop & Go!
C2

Hot Potato Wide ClearWIDE

Save, beat the 4-second count, clear to the wide sideline alley.

Goal

  • Skill: clear the crease fast and wide
  • Why: a save is wasted if the ball stays in the kitchen
  • Watch: feet leave the crease, ball goes wide not up the middle

Setup & Ball

Mark the crease with cones; outlet target out near the sideline. Coach feeds a Swax Lax save. Group counts “ONE-potato… OUT!” Save, step out, clear wide to the sideline — never up the middle.

2-Person

Backup goalie / parent: is the wide outlet AND the official counter — receives the clear by the sideline, rolls it back. Swap each round.

BeginnerSlow count. Just: feet OUT of the crease, ball tossed to the wide side.
AdvancedReal-tempo count. Clear the crease AND complete the wide sideline pass before “OUT.” Add a 2nd wide target.

Mastery Ladder

L1 · IntroductionSteps out of the crease after the save
L2 · CompetentClears toward the wide side before the count ends
L3 · Game ReadyBeats the 4-count with a completed wide pass ~7 of 10

Coaching Cues

Hot Potato!Feet Out!Clear It Wide!
C3

Full Play Live Rep

Everything at tempo: track, step, save, hunt, wide clear — one rep.

Goal

  • Skill: link the whole sequence at game speed
  • Why: real reps tie all the pieces together
  • Watch: completes each step in order under light pressure

Setup & Ball

Full crease + sideline outlet. Coach takes a light, announced shot. Real-ball gate: stay on Swax Lax until the goalie catches 8 of 10 without turning his head or closing his eyes — only then a light real ball. When in doubt, stay soft.

2-Person

Backup goalie / parent: plays the outlet attacker breaking to open space near the sideline for the wide pass, then feeds back to the coach. Rotate goalies every 6 full reps.

BeginnerAnnounced medium Swax Lax straight on, one wide target. All steps in order — not speed.
AdvancedLight real-ball to varied zones. Read it, step, save, hunt any rebound, clear wide to a moving outlet.

Mastery Ladder

L1 · IntroductionCompletes save-then-clear in order, slowly, with help
L2 · CompetentStrings track→save→clear together on his own on Swax Lax
L3 · Game ReadyRuns the full play on light real balls, ~half clean, talks through it

Coaching Cues

See It, Stop It!Hunt & Clear Wide!Whole Play!
D

Crease Command & Communication

A goalie who talks beats one who doesn’t.

D1

Ball-Top CallerTALK

Learn the four basic location calls and say them loud, every time.

Goal

  • Skill: call the ball’s location — Ball Top, Left, Right
  • Why: a talking goalie is worth more than a quiet one at this age
  • Watch: loud, confident, calls without being reminded

Setup & Ball

No shots needed at first. Coach (or 2–3 helpers) holds a ball and moves around the top of the box. Goalie points and calls “Ball Top!”, “Left!”, “Right!” as the ball moves. Add ”Check!” when the coach enters the scoring zone with hands free — a pre-shot readiness cue, not a reaction to the wind-up.

2-Person

Backup goalie / parent: acts as a second/third ‘attacker’ moving the ball, and gives a thumbs-up every time the call is loud enough. Swap the talker every 1–2 minutes.

BeginnerCoach moves slowly; goalie calls the spot when prompted (“where’s the ball?”).
AdvancedCoach moves quickly between spots; goalie calls on his own, adds “Check!” as the threat forms — feet set, hands free — not at the release.

Mastery Ladder

L1 · IntroductionCalls location and “Check!” (as the threat forms, not on release) when prompted by the coach
L2 · CompetentCalls Left / Right / Ball Top on his own as the ball moves
L3 · Game ReadyTalks through most of a moving drill loudly without being told

Coaching Cues

Ball Top!Left! Right!Loud & Proud!
D2

Save & Shout BreakTALK

Tie talking to the save — read the threat early, then call the break.

Goal

  • Skill: “Check!” on the threat before the shot, “Break!” after the save, “One More!” on a miss
  • Why: links his voice to the action so talking becomes automatic
  • Watch: calls “Check!” as the attacker threatens — feet set, hands free — before the release

Setup & Ball

Light Swax Lax shots. Goalie calls ”Check!” as the coach steps into shooting position with hands free — before the wind-up — makes the save, then yells ”Break!” and outlets wide. If a shot misses the net, he calls ”One More!” to reset the defense.

2-Person

Backup goalie / parent: is the outlet who only moves when he HEARS “Break!” — so a quiet break gets no help. Teaches him the voice has a purpose. Rotate every 8 reps.

BeginnerCoach prompts each call. Save first, then add the “Break!” shout.
AdvancedGoalie calls “Check!” as the threat forms (before release), saves, breaks — full cycle on his own, adds “One More!” on misses.

Mastery Ladder

L1 · IntroductionShouts “Break!” after the save when reminded
L2 · CompetentCalls “Check!” as the threat forms (not on release) and “Break!” after — unprompted most reps
L3 · Game ReadyReads the threat, calls "Check!", saves, calls "Break!" (or "One More!") — every rep, unprompted

Coaching Cues

Check!One More!Break!

Practice Shape & Ball Rule

Kids hold focus ~3–4 min per activity. Keep blocks short.

Each session: gear check → tracking (4m) → footwork (4m) → save/clear or comm block (6m) → finish on a mini-game.

Ball rule — earned, not assumed: tennis ball → Swax Lax → real ball, only after the no-flinch gate (8 of 10 caught without turning his head or closing his eyes). When in doubt, stay soft.

Solo Engagement Games

No backup goalie? He competes against a number. Under 5 minutes, always ends on a win.

🎯 Beat Your Number

Races his own best — the only opponent is yesterday.

  • 10 announced soft shots; count every ball he gets a body or stick on (touches count).
  • That number is his “record” — write it on the stick tape.
  • Beat it by one next time = team cheer.

🏰 Don't Wake the Dragon

The net is a sleeping dragon’s cave — keep it asleep.

  • Starts with 5 “sleep” points; each save keeps the dragon asleep.
  • A goal “wakes” it — lose a point with a fun roar so a goal is silly, not scary.
  • Survive 8 shots with points left = tamed it. Then add a wide clear.
