## Design Context

### Users
Single user: the head coach. Pulled up on a phone (max-width 560px) at practice, between drill reps, often outdoors in bright sunlight. Needs to scan fast — no time to hunt for information. Zero parent-facing use; this is a coaching tool with authority and directness, not a welcoming parent portal.

### Brand Personality
**Team**: Blue Claws Lacrosse · Ages 7–9 · goalie program  
**Three words**: Energetic, scrappy, loud  
**Logo signals**: Two assets — a classic sports crest (blue lobster claw + red lacrosse stick head, bold black outlines) and a hype mascot (cartoonish lacrosse ball character, backwards cap, dripping blue/red paint, peace sign). Both are bold, graphic, American youth sports.  
**Emotional goal**: The coach should feel equipped and confident. The app should feel like a pro coaching resource, not a DIY Google Doc.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Theme**: Dark. Coaches use this outdoors in sun — dark reduces glare. Dark is also the natural register for sports apps (ESPN, NFL, lacrosse team apps). The current green was field-thematic; it doesn't reflect brand identity.

- Accent: Red — oklch(48% 0.20 25) — lacrosse stick, energy, urgency
- White/light text: oklch(93% 0.015 262) — blue-tinted near-white
- Background: oklch(16% 0.025 262) — deep navy, not pure black
- Surface (cards): oklch(21% 0.03 262) — slightly lifted navy
- Border: oklch(28% 0.04 262)

Pod category colors (keep semantic meaning, shift to work on dark):
- Tracking: Royal blue (already matches team color)
- Footwork: Amber/gold — oklch(72% 0.16 75)
- Clearing: Red-orange — oklch(58% 0.22 38)
- Communication: Teal — oklch(58% 0.14 185)

**Anti-palette**: Do NOT use the current green (#0f1c14) as dominant surface color — it reads as "soccer" or "generic field sport," not Blue Claws. Do NOT use cyan/purple/neon gradients (AI tells). Do NOT use glassmorphism.

**Typography** (after running the font selection procedure):
- Brand words: punchy · athletic · loud
- Reflex fonts rejected: Barlow Condensed, Oswald, Bebas Neue (too clichéd for sports)
- Display/headings: **Saira Condensed** (ExtraBold, 800) — narrow, structured, authoritative; reads like a jersey nameplate or a coach's whiteboard heading. Not a typical AI pick for sports.
- Body: **Readex Pro** — readable at 12–14px on dark mobile screens; has breadth across weights without being Inter
- Monospace accents (drill codes like A1, B3): **Azeret Mono** — for the numbered identifiers only, creates a "gear" or "system" feel

**References**: No external references provided. Direction: think Sports Reference (the stats site) meets a premium team's playbook PDF — dense, confident, data-forward.
**Anti-references**: Generic admin dashboards, rounded card stacks with pastel pill badges, anything that could be a SaaS product homepage.

### Design Principles

1. **Scan speed above all.** The coach is mid-practice. Headers, labels, and cues must be immediately readable at arm's length. Hierarchy must be brutal — one dominant thing per visual zone.

2. **The team colors are load-bearing.** Royal blue and red are not accent colors here — they define sections, states, and identity. Don't dilute them with too many competing colors.

3. **Dense but not cluttered.** Coaching references pack a lot in. Embrace density where content demands it (mastery tables, drill steps), but use generous breathing room in section headers and navigation.

4. **Athletic authority, not playful.** The user is the coach, not the player. The mascot energy is for the team brand; the app energy is for the professional tool the coach pulls out. Bold, confident, structured.

5. **Every detail earns its place.** The SVG field diagrams, mastery ladders, coaching cues, and pod chips are all genuinely functional. Don't add decoration for decoration's sake. If it's not a coaching decision, cut it.
